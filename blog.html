How varied is the cost of basic amenities between cities?

Student Project | November 2022

Context

Having lived in India, Italy and now the USA, I have always been amazed about how different the costs are for food, housing, and other basic amenities between these countries. These costs are all tied to daily wages which then impact the overall cost of living. In order to have a comparative overview, I created a visualization that showcases the costs of select amenities in select cities/ states of India, Italy and USA.

Interact with Visualization

Data Set Selected

I used a dataset from Kaggle that contains information about the cost of living in almost 5000 cities across the world.
This data set contains prices for 54 variables. For my visualization, I first cleaned the datset on python to focus on select 6 variables and 10 cities.

Data Manipulation In Python

Data Cleaning - Variable Selection

I first shortlisted the 10 cities/regions I would be including in my visualization based on my personal experiences and interactions with the place. Starting with home in India (Mumbai) and Italy (Milan) where I lived for 3 years for work. For USA, I decided to take 2 states each from the 4 regions of USA. I selected California, a place I visit often to be with my husband, Boston which is where I am studying, and Michigan which is where I lived for a short time when my husband was in grad school.

For the variable, I again delved into my personal experience and listed amenities where I found a visible difference in the costs across geographies (including minimum wage). I grouped them under 4 main categories based on the variables of the original dataset.

Final Dataset for Visualization

Vizualization Seleted - Dotplot

I chose to represent the data using a variant of lollipop chart, the dotplot. Dotplots give the flexibility to compare 2 values of data for several entities, which is the geography in this case. While the Y-axis showcases the city/state, the X-axis showcases the price in USD for the 2 data points selected using the buttons. Using a dotplot, I could represent multiple data points on a single chart without it being too cluttered and overwhelming.
